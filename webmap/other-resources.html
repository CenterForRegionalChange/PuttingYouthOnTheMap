Sites Putting Youth-Relevant Data on Maps

At UCDCRC, Putting Youth On the Map provides analyses of youth well-being, vulnerability to inadequate support, access to adequate financial resources, and youth voting, available by race/ethnicity and sex.

・The Regional Opportunity Index provides analyses of opportunities available for youth, families and communities. The site currently provides maps for California’s Central Valley; it will eventually expand statewide.

・Kidsdata.org includes data about the health and well being of children across California. The site offers data for every city, county, and school district in the state.

・ChildrenNow.org osts the 2010 California County Scorecard of Children's Well-Being. It tracks 26 interrelated indicators of children's well-being for each of California's 58 counties.

・HealthyCity.org provides current data, maps, and service referrals for California at many different geographic levels, including census block group and school district, on an online platform.

・The National Center for Education Statistics mapping tool allows users to view maps of states and school districts, while overlaying statistics on population and housing, race and ethnicity, economics and social characteristics.

・ Query CHKS makes it possible to generate tables, maps, graphs and charts from the California Healthy Kids Survey Data.

・Community Commons allows you to make maps with existing data, upload your own data and generate community health reports.

・This interactive mapping tool allows users to locate federally supported youth programs in a community.

・Policy Map lets users create maps using public data layers that can be shared and saved.

・DiversityData and DiversityDataKids provide maps with various equity analyses.

・Office of Environmental Health Hazard Assessment provides data and data maps related to hazardous environmental exposures.

・SmartLocation Database provides interactive maps and data for measuring the built environment.

・U.S. Environmental Protection Agency’s Enviroatlas provides information about ecosystem health, including clean air/water, recreational opportunities, and protection from severe weather.

・This KidsRights Index is the first global ranking on how countries world-wide are adhering to children’s rights.

Other Sites that are also putting youth data on the map

・AskCHIS allows you to search for health statistics on your county, region and stat.

・Health Profiles present CHIS estimates to track changes in insurance status, disease, health behaviors and overall health status over time.

・The California Healthy Kids Resource Center maintains links to data sources and interactive data tools.

・American FactFinder is a portal to U.S. Census Bureau datasets and economic surveys. You can map the data or download it.

・The California Department of Education’s DataQuest provides reports about California’s schools and school districts.

・UCLA Center for Health Policy Research and the California Center for Public Health Advocacy provide city level data on overweight and obesity in California in this report.

・California Vital Statistics website allows the download of birth and death records by ZIP code from the California Department of Public Health records system.

・SAMHSA, the Substance Abuse and Mental Health Services Administration of the US Dept. of Health and Human Services provides data on substance use and mental health in California and the US.

・County Health Rankings and Roadmaps (Robert Wood Johnson Foundation).

・Child Welfare Dynamic Report System provides child abuse and foster care rates for California, as well as other data on children and youth in the foster care system.

・Centers for Disease Control and Prevention: Youth Risk behavior surveillance system (YRBSS).

・Child Trends “Flourishing Children Project” provides information about positive indicators of child development and an emerging national dataset.

・W. Haywood-Burns Institute provides data on racial disparities in the juvenile justice system.

・The Department of Justice provides data about arrests, crime and incarceration.

・The California Adolescent Health Data Set provides data about the health status of adolescents.

Put Yourself on the Map

The data and maps on these websites present a very partial story about California’s youth and communities. Many challenges remain undocumented, and many resources are not reflected. Some information might be inaccurate. To fill out this picture, we need young people and youth allies to put their knowledge on the map.

・Some youth have been doing just that in California’s Capital Region with the CRC using the Youth Story Map.

・Other youth are involved in similar efforts around the world. For example, see UNICEF-GIS.

Here are a variety of websites that support people creating their own maps.

・Crowdmap is site for finding and sharing information as well as creating maps.

・Ushahidi provides free and open source software for information collection, visualization of data and interactive mapping.

・MapStory is a platform for creating, sharing and collaborating stories to understand global dynamics over the course of history.

・Map Your World will be a multi-platform project where young people will be able to use new technologies to map, track and improve the condition of their communities.

・Google Map Maker is a mapping site that uses crowdsourcing.

・OpenStreatMap is a platform that creates and provides free geographic data.

・StatPlanet offers Interactive Mapping and Visualization Software.

・Click2Map is a free onlne map creation tool.

・ZeeMaps is a tool for creating and publishing interactive maps.

・iMapBuilderOnline is a tool for creating interactive flash and google maps in web browsers.

・Mangomap is a tool for creating interactive web maps.
